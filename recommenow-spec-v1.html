The Problem
The credential that doesn't exist yet

Every hiring process runs on trust. But the tools we use to establish that trust are broken — in predictable, unfair ways that disadvantage exactly the people who most need help.

Two equally capable candidates apply for the same role. One has an internal referral. The other doesn't. The first gets the interview. This isn't about skill — it's about access to social proof at the right moment. RecommeNow exists to fix this asymmetry.
Core thesis · RecommeNow v1.0

LinkedIn recommendations are buried, unstructured, un-embeddable, and ignored after two seconds.

References only get called at the end of the process — after the first impression is already made. Too late.

Skill endorsements are clicked by strangers and treated as noise.

CV claims are entirely self-reported, with zero external validation.

RecommeNow is none of these. It's a structured, portable, peer-verified reputation profile — built from people who actually worked with you, visible from the very first moment someone looks you up.

The strategic long game

LinkedIn will not be integrated with immediately — and that's intentional. When RecommeNow is valuable enough, they will come to us. The near-term play is a URL in LinkedIn's About section and optimised Open Graph cards that create a rich preview when the link is shared. We don't need their permission to be useful. We need users.

2
Founder Decisions
Every open question, answered

These decisions are final for v1. They are not to be relitigated during build. If circumstances change, revisit in v2 planning.

Question
LinkedIn integration depth — do we build a native widget via their API?
Decision ✓
No native integration in v1. We build audience first. When we're big enough, they come to us and open the API. Short-term: optimised OG cards + URL in About section. That's sufficient to drive traffic and demonstrate value.
Question
Who moderates flagged vouches — founder-led or async queue?
Decision ✓
Async review queue with a 48-hour SLA. All flagged vouches enter a review queue. The vouch giver is notified and asked to re-confirm. Resolution within 48 hours — either reinstated with a note or removed. Process is documented and visible in the public Trust Policy page.
Question
Are custom profile slugs free or a Pro feature?
Decision ✓
Custom slugs are Pro only. Free users get a functional auto-generated URL. Pro users can set any available slug. This creates a genuine upgrade incentive — a clean, memorable URL is worth paying for when you're actively job searching.
Question
What about negative feedback — should it be allowed?
Decision ✓
In v1, users control what's shown. You choose who you ask, and you approve what appears on your profile. This mirrors exactly how professional references work — nobody expects you to list people who'll speak against you. The FAQ addresses this transparently.
Question
What's the domain?
Decision ✓
recommenow.com — to be registered this week, before a single line of code is written. Nothing else ships before the domain is secured.
Note on negative feedback — the FAQ answer

"RecommeNow works like a professional reference. You choose who you ask, and nobody expects you to list someone who'll speak against you. What makes this more trustworthy than anonymous reviews is that every vouch giver's name and company is publicly visible — they're putting their own professional reputation on the line when they recommend you."

3
Trust Architecture
How we stop fake vouches

The hardest problem and the most important one to get right. A platform whose feedback can be gamed is worse than useless — it actively misleads the people we're trying to help. The trust model is layered, transparent, and honest about uncertainty.

1
Work email domain verification
Vouch givers must verify via a work email before their vouch publishes. sarah@stripe.com confirms Stripe employment — checkable, attributable, real. Personal Gmail gets a lower trust tier, displayed transparently. This alone eliminates casual fraud and raises the cost of dishonesty significantly.
Low friction · Free
2
Velocity & burst detection
More than 3 vouches from the same IP cluster, device fingerprint, or email domain within 4 hours triggers a hold. Natural vouch patterns are sparse and spread over days and weeks. Burst submissions are the clearest signal of coordinated gaming. Held vouches are queued and reviewed within 48 hours.
Automated · Always on
3
Public accountability by default — the strongest deterrent
Every vouch giver's name, job title, and company is displayed publicly on the profile. No anonymous feedback accepted — ever. This is the single most powerful fraud prevention in the system. People do not misrepresent their professional identity when their real reputation is attached to the claim.
Social pressure · Most effective
4
Background plausibility scoring
A lightweight check: does the stated role + company + relationship type form a coherent picture? A "Founder" from a solo company claiming to be someone's "direct report" is incoherent. These signals weight the trust score quietly — no user-facing warnings, just honest accounting in the backend.
Automated · Background
5
Community flagging — recruiter-powered quality control
Any profile visitor can flag a vouch as suspicious. Three independent flags trigger a 48-hour hold. The vouch giver receives an email asking to re-confirm. This crowdsources quality control from exactly the audience with the most to lose if the system is gamed — recruiters and hiring managers.
Community-driven · 48h SLA
6
Transparent trust tiers on every vouch
Rather than hiding uncertainty, every vouch shows its verification level. This honesty builds more recruiter trust than a system pretending everything is perfectly verified. A platform that acknowledges uncertainty is more credible than one making false guarantees.
Transparent · Trust-building
🟢 Work email verified
Confirmed via work domain. Highest confidence. Displayed prominently. This is what recruiters weight most.
🟡 Self-declared, unverified
Personal email used. Name and role self-reported. Visible on profile with a clear label — recruiter judges its weight.
🔴 Flagged — under review
Hidden from public profile. Vouch giver notified. Resolved within 48 hours. Outcome communicated to both parties.
4
Product Surfaces
Three surfaces, one source of truth

The profile is the data model. The feedback form writes to it. The embeds read from it. Everything else is presentation.

01
Feedback Collection Form
Mobile-optimised. Sent by the candidate to their network. Giver states name, title, company, relationship type. Selects up to 6 traits from 16. Writes 2–4 sentences. Star rating. Work email verification before publish. No account needed to give a vouch — zero friction for givers.
Target: 60 seconds to complete
02
Public Reputation Profile
A public page at recommenow.com/[slug]. Overall trust score, relationship diversity indicator, top traits ranked by mention frequency, all verified vouches with trust tier badges, and a one-click add-vouch button. This is what a recruiter lands on when the link is shared.
The core product · drives all growth
03
Portable Embeds
Three formats: an embeddable HTML widget for portfolios and personal sites (auto-updates live), a shareable badge URL for LinkedIn About, email signatures, and CV headers, and a PDF one-pager generated on demand for applications that don't accept URLs.
Pro feature — unlocks at £7/mo
5
URLs & Profile Slugs
Every user gets a link. Pro users get the clean one.

Free users receive a fully functional, shareable URL from day one — there's no barrier to sharing your profile. The Pro upgrade delivers a clean, memorable custom slug that looks better in a CV header, LinkedIn, or email signature.

Free recommenow.com/nick-b-a4f2 Auto-generated · functional · shareable
Pro recommenow.com/nickb Custom · clean · memorable · £7/mo
Pro recommenow.com/nick-baker Custom · any available slug
Why this works as a growth mechanic

A free user who lands a job and comes back for their next search will upgrade to Pro immediately — because they've seen the profile work, they know recruiters visited it, and a clean URL is a small cost for a meaningful signal of seriousness. Job searching is high-stakes, time-limited, and people will pay to look more credible.

Tech Stack
Built to launch fast, scale when it works

Every tool chosen for MVP has a generous free tier. Total cost at zero-to-low traffic: ~£0–30/month. Only pay when it's working.

Frontend
Next.js 14 (App Router) — feedback form, profile pages, dashboard. SEO-friendly for public profiles — critical for recruiter discoverability.
Auth
Clerk — candidate signup via email magic link + Google OAuth. Free tier covers MVP comfortably.
Database
Supabase (Postgres) — profiles, vouches, verification states, flags, review queue. Row-level security built in. Free tier covers first 1,000 users.
Email
Resend — verification emails, vouch notifications, flag alerts, 48h review SLA notifications. 3,000 emails/month free.
Rate limits
Upstash Redis — burst detection and velocity checks. Serverless, per-request pricing. Near-zero cost at MVP scale.
PDF gen
React-PDF — on-demand one-pager from profile data. Runs as a serverless function. Pro feature only.
Embed widget
Vanilla JS snippet — single <script> tag, fetches profile via public API, renders inline. Zero React dependency for end users' sites.
Hosting
Vercel — zero-config Next.js deployment. Edge functions for widget API. Free to start, scales automatically.
Payments
Stripe — Pro subscriptions + Recruiter plan billing. Only needed for Phase 2 when monetisation begins.

Product Principles
The rules we don't break
⚖️
Honesty over the illusion of certainty
We never claim every vouch is verified. We show trust tiers honestly and let recruiters weigh the evidence themselves. A transparent system is more credible than a system that makes false guarantees.
🚪
Zero friction for givers
Giving a vouch must never require creating an account. Friction on the giver side kills the product. The candidate gets the account. The giver gets a 60-second form and a thank-you.
🧍
Every vouch is a real person with a name
No anonymous feedback. Ever. Public accountability is not a feature — it is the foundation of the trust model. If someone isn't willing to put their name to it, it doesn't belong here.
🛂
Candidates control what they show
You choose who you ask. You approve what appears. This mirrors real references — it's not a weakness, it's the design. The FAQ explains this plainly. We do not apologise for it.
📦
Build audience before building integrations
No platform integrations until the product proves its own value. LinkedIn, ATS systems, and job boards will become available to us when we have leverage. Leverage comes from users, not from API keys.
🌐
The profile is the product
Everything else — the form, the widgets, the PDF — exists to create and distribute the profile. If a feature doesn't make the profile more valuable or more reachable, it waits for v2.
